Chocolate Cake

Description

This is the ultimate classic chocolate cake: rich, moist, and deeply chocolaty, topped with a velvety smooth chocolate ganache or buttercream. It's surprisingly easy to bake and consistently delivers a show-stopping dessert that's perfect for birthdays, celebrations, or simply satisfying a serious chocolate craving.

The recipe uses simple ingredients to create a tender crumb and intense flavor. Once you try this homemade version, you'll never go back to store-bought. Get ready for rave reviews!

Ingredients

For the Cake:

  • 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 3/4 cups granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup buttermilk (or milk with 1 tbsp vinegar/lemon juice)
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup hot water (or hot coffee for deeper flavor)

For the Chocolate Frosting (or Ganache):

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 4 cups powdered sugar, sifted
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Steps

  1. **Prepare Cake Batter:** Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 8-inch round cake pans. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. In a separate bowl, whisk together buttermilk, oil, eggs, and vanilla extract. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until just combined. Stir in the hot water (or coffee) until smooth. The batter will be thin.
  2. **Bake Cakes:** Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a wooden skewer inserted into the center comes out clean. Let cakes cool in pans for 10 minutes before inverting onto a wire rack to cool completely.
  3. **Make Frosting:** While cakes cool, prepare frosting. In a large bowl, beat softened butter until creamy. Gradually beat in cocoa powder, then powdered sugar alternately with milk, starting and ending with powdered sugar. Beat until smooth and creamy. Stir in vanilla extract and salt. If too thick, add more milk 1 tsp at a time; if too thin, add more powdered sugar.
  4. **Assemble Cake:** Once cakes are completely cool, place one cake layer on a serving plate. Spread about 1/3 of the frosting evenly over the top. Place the second cake layer on top and spread the remaining frosting over the top and sides of the cake.
  5. **Serve:** Slice and serve! The cake can be stored at room temperature in an airtight container for up to 3 days.
